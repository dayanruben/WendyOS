## Installing WendyOS on NVIDIA Jetson

This guide will walk you through installing WendyOS on supported NVIDIA Jetson devices. WendyOS supports Jetson Orin Nano today, AGX Orin support is imminent, and AGX Thor support is in progress.

For DGX Spark, use [Wendy Agent — Linux](/docs/installation/wendy-agent-linux) on the existing OS instead of flashing WendyOS.

Prefer to follow along with video? Watch the [WendyOS NVIDIA Jetson Orin Nano installation video](https://youtu.be/TtDJAtBUEZI).

### Installation Steps

<Steps>
  <Step>
    ### Connect the NVMe SSD to Your Computer

    Using an NVMe SSD to USB-C adapter, connect your NVMe SSD into the adapter and then the USB-C end to your MacBook.

    <video width="100%" autoPlay loop muted playsInline>
      <source src="/videos/installation/jetson-nvme-mac-adapter.mp4" type="video/mp4" />
      Your browser does not support the video tag.
    </video>

    On macOS you'll be presented with a permission dialog to use the adapter. Press **Allow**.
  </Step>

  <Step>
    ### Install WendyOS

    Use the `wendy` CLI to install the operating system:

    ```bash
    wendy install
    ```

    Select **NVIDIA Jetson Orin Nano** and then select your drive. You'll begin a process of downloading a copy of WendyOS to your hard drive. This could take a while but soon you'll be prompted to put WendyOS on the NVMe SSD.

    You'll need to enter your user's administrator password for this to continue.

    During the install, the CLI also prompts you to set up first-boot provisioning. You can:

    - **Connect to WiFi on first boot** — pre-seed one or more networks so the device joins WiFi the moment it powers on, no monitor or keyboard required.
    - **Install important updates on first boot** — the device fetches and applies any critical OS updates automatically the first time it boots.
    - **Securely link the device to Wendy Cloud or your developer machine** — pre-enroll the device (`--pre-enroll`, requires `wendy auth login`) so it provisions mTLS certificates and links to Wendy Cloud on first boot. It links to your developer machine once you connect it over USB-C and run `wendy discover`.

    Each of these is optional and can also be configured after the device boots. See [`wendy os install`](/docs/advanced/clients/wendy-cli/commands/os/install) for all the flags.
  </Step>

  <Step>
    ### Install the NVMe SSD into Your Jetson

    Remove the NVMe SSD from your computer and insert it into your NVIDIA Jetson Orin Nano on the M.2 slot on the bottom of the board.

    <video width="100%" autoPlay loop muted playsInline>
      <source src="/videos/installation/jetson-nvme-bottom-insertion.mp4" type="video/mp4" />
      Your browser does not support the video tag.
    </video>
  </Step>

  <Step>
    ### Connect the Jetson to Your Computer

    Connect your NVIDIA Jetson Orin Nano to your computer using a USB-C cable. This will allow you to deploy and manage apps on your device.

    <video width="100%" autoPlay loop muted playsInline>
      <source src="/videos/installation/jetson-mac-usb-cable.mp4" type="video/mp4" />
      Your browser does not support the video tag.
    </video>
  </Step>

  <Step>
    ### Power On the Device

    With the supplied power supply, insert the power into the power slot on the NVIDIA Jetson Orin Nano and then plug into a power source like your wall outlet.

    <video width="100%" autoPlay loop muted playsInline>
      <source src="/videos/installation/jetson-power-cable-insertion.mp4" type="video/mp4" />
      Your browser does not support the video tag.
    </video>
  </Step>

  <Step>
    ### Discover Your Device

    Wait 1-2 minutes after powering on your Jetson to allow WendyOS to fully boot. Then run:

    ```bash
    wendy discover
    ```

    You should see your NVIDIA Jetson Orin Nano appear in the list of discovered devices.

    ![Wendy Discover](/images/docs/wendy-discover.png)

    Your device is now ready for development! For more information on device discovery and configuration, see [Discovering Devices](/docs/guides/device/discovering-devices).
  </Step>
</Steps>
