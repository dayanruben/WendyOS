## Installing WendyOS on Raspberry Pi 5

This guide will walk you through installing WendyOS on your Raspberry Pi 5. The Wendy agent comes prepackaged with WendyOS, so no separate agent installation is required.

Prefer to follow along with video? Watch the [WendyOS Raspberry Pi 5 installation video](https://youtu.be/87GlYkx86jY).

### Installation Steps

<Steps>
  <Step>
    ### Connect the Micro SD Card to Your Computer

    Insert your micro SD card into a micro SD to SD card adapter.

    <video width="100%" autoPlay loop muted playsInline>
      <source src="/videos/installation/pi-5-micro-sd-to-sd-card-adapter.mp4" type="video/mp4" />
      Your browser does not support the video tag.
    </video>

    Then insert the adapter into your MacBook's SD card slot.

    <video width="100%" autoPlay loop muted playsInline>
      <source src="/videos/installation/pi-5-insert-card-into-macbook.mp4" type="video/mp4" />
      Your browser does not support the video tag.
    </video>
  </Step>

  <Step>
    ### Install WendyOS

    Use the `wendy` CLI to install the operating system:

    ```bash
    wendy os install
    ```

    Select **Raspberry Pi 5** and then select your drive. You'll begin a process of downloading a copy of WendyOS to your SD card. This could take a while but soon you'll be prompted to put WendyOS on the micro SD card.

    You'll need to enter your user's administrator password for this to continue.
  </Step>

  <Step>
    ### Insert the Micro SD Card into Your Pi

    Remove the SD card from your computer and insert the micro SD card into your Raspberry Pi 5.

    <video width="100%" autoPlay loop muted playsInline>
      <source src="/videos/installation/pi-5-insert-micro-sd-card-to-pi.mp4" type="video/mp4" />
      Your browser does not support the video tag.
    </video>
  </Step>

  <Step>
    ### Connect the Pi to Your Computer

    Connect your Raspberry Pi 5 to your computer using a USB-C cable. This will allow you to deploy and manage apps on your device.

    <video width="100%" autoPlay loop muted playsInline>
      <source src="/videos/installation/pi-5-usb-c-to-macbook.mp4" type="video/mp4" />
      Your browser does not support the video tag.
    </video>
  </Step>

  <Step>
    ### Discover Your Device

    Wait 1-2 minutes after powering on your Pi to allow WendyOS to fully boot. Then run:

    ```bash
    wendy discover
    # Set this device as your default
    wendy device set-default
    ```

    You should see your Raspberry Pi 5 appear in the list of discovered devices.

    ![Wendy Discover](/images/docs/wendy-discover.png)

    Your device is now ready for development! For more information on device discovery and configuration, see [Discovering Devices](/docs/guides/device/discovering-devices).
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Next Steps

Now that WendyOS is installed, you can start deploying apps to your device. Check out the [Hello World Guide](/docs/guides/python/hello-world) to build your first app.
