---
title: NVIDIA Jetson AGX Thor
description: Install WendyOS on an NVIDIA Jetson AGX Thor over USB recovery
---

Jetson AGX Thor uses a dedicated USB recovery flash path. WendyOS flashes the Thor's QSPI and internal NVMe directly; no external drive is required.

## What You Need

- NVIDIA Jetson AGX Thor Developer Kit
- USB-C data cable for your MacBook or other development machine
- Supplied Thor power supply
- Wendy CLI installed on your development machine

## Installation Steps

<Steps>
  <Step>
    ### Connect the Cables and Enter Recovery Mode

    Start with the Thor powered off. Turn it so the **back faces you**; you should see all of the ports. Connect the USB-C cable from your MacBook to the **right** USB-C port, next to HDMI.

    <video width="100%" autoPlay loop muted playsInline>
      <source src="/videos/installation/jetson-thor-usb-c-connection.mp4" type="video/mp4" />
      Your browser does not support the video tag.
    </video>

    Plug the power-supply cable into a power outlet, then connect it to the **left** USB-C port on the Thor.

    <video width="100%" autoPlay loop muted playsInline>
      <source src="/videos/installation/jetson-thor-power-connection.mp4" type="video/mp4" />
      Your browser does not support the video tag.
    </video>

    Turn the Thor so the **front faces you**. You should see three small buttons and an LED light.

    1. Press and **hold** the **middle** button.
    2. Tap the **right** button.
    3. Wait a moment, then release the **middle** button.

    <video width="100%" autoPlay loop muted playsInline>
      <source src="/videos/installation/jetson-thor-recovery-mode.mp4" type="video/mp4" />
      Your browser does not support the video tag.
    </video>

    You should hear the fans quiet down. The Thor is now ready to flash.
  </Step>

  <Step>
    ### Install WendyOS

    Run the installer from your terminal:

    ```bash
    sudo wendy os install
    ```

    Follow the on-screen instructions. The CLI downloads a Thor flashpack, detects the Thor in USB recovery mode, and flashes QSPI plus its internal NVMe.

    <video width="100%" autoPlay loop muted playsInline>
      <source src="/videos/installation/jetson-thor-flash-cli.mp4" type="video/mp4" />
      Your browser does not support the video tag.
    </video>
  </Step>

  <Step>
    ### Boot WendyOS

    After the CLI reports a successful flash, power-cycle the Thor out of recovery mode. It will boot WendyOS from internal storage; leave the data-capable USB-C cable connected to your computer.
  </Step>

  <Step>
    ### Discover Your Device

    Wait 1-2 minutes for WendyOS to boot, then run:

    ```bash
    wendy discover
    ```

    <img
      src={`${(process.env.NEXT_PUBLIC_BASE_PATH || '').replace(/\/$/, '')}/images/docs/cli/discover/devices-thor-dark.png`}
      alt="The Wendy CLI listing a discovered NVIDIA Jetson AGX Thor on the network"
      style={{ width: '100%' }}
    />

    Your Thor is ready for development. See [Discovering Devices](/docs/device/discovering-devices) to learn more.
  </Step>
</Steps>
